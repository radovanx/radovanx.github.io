Self-Belief and Strategic Optimism

The most successful people are “a little delusional” — not reckless, but possessing unwavering conviction in their ability to figure things out. This insight from Addy Osmani (Google) distills a pattern visible across builders and leaders.

Key Ideas

Self-doubt is self-perpetuating. Your brain actively works to confirm negative beliefs (Ross Harkness). The inverse is also true — positive conviction shapes reality by directing attention and effort toward solutions rather than obstacles.

Talent is not the differentiator. Those who ship, build, and lead aren’t necessarily the most talented. They’re the ones who refuse to let their inner critic win.

No prerequisites needed. No permission, no perfect conditions, no universal support. The only requirement is genuine belief in your capability to navigate challenges.

Takeaway

Strategic optimism functions as fuel — it enables persistence when logic alone would suggest stopping. The “delusion” is functional: it keeps you in the game long enough for competence to catch up with ambition.
